Does Treasury debt management offset or amplify the effects of Fed QT?

Under an ample-reserves regime, the effective monetary stance depends jointly on Fed balance-sheet runoff, Treasury issuance composition, and private intermediation capacity. This project follows the balance sheets.

The Great Drainage

Balance Sheet

When the Fed lets Treasuries roll off its balance sheet, someone else must hold them. Meanwhile, the ON RRP facility — which once absorbed $2.5 trillion of excess liquidity — has drained to near zero. These two forces define the pressure on the private sector.

Who Absorbs the Duration?

Dealers

Primary dealers are the first line of absorption. As SOMA declines and Treasury continues issuing, dealer inventories have more than doubled.

During QT2, the correlation between SOMA decline and dealer inventory buildup is r = −0.89. Dealers absorb what the Fed sheds.

What Does It Cost?

Repo Market

Repo spreads measure the price of overnight Treasury financing relative to the Fed’s administered rates. As the ON RRP buffer drains and dealer balance sheets fill, spreads have widened.

Loading repo data…

During QT2, repo spreads correlate r = 0.83 with dealer inventories and r = −0.80 with system liquidity. As the buffer drains, funding costs rise.

Treasury’s Issuance Response

Duration

Net private duration supply measures how much duration the private sector must absorb: coupon issuance + SOMA runoff − buybacks. When Treasury launched its buyback program in May 2024, it began actively removing duration from the market.

Loading quarterly data…

By 2025Q2, net private duration supply turned negative — buybacks were removing more duration than new issuance and SOMA runoff were adding. The green bars show quarters where Treasury was net-removing duration from private markets.

The Buffer

ON RRP

The ON RRP facility acted as a liquidity buffer during early QT — money market funds parked cash there instead of in reserves. As the buffer drains, the system becomes more sensitive to further drainage.
