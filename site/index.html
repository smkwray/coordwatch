How do Fed runoff, Treasury debt management, and liquidity conditions combine under ample reserves?

Under an ample-reserves regime, monetary conditions depend jointly on Fed balance-sheet runoff, Treasury issuance composition, TGA management, ON RRP drainage, and private intermediation capacity. Read the site in that order: drainage and buffers first, then cash mechanics, duration burden, intermediation, sectoral absorption, and only then the supplementary regressions.

The Great Drainage

Balance Sheet

When the Fed lets Treasuries roll off its balance sheet, someone else must hold them. Meanwhile, the ON RRP facility — which once absorbed $2.5 trillion of excess liquidity — has drained to near zero. These two forces define the pressure on the private sector.
